## Overview

Built as part of my **IFS 315 Emerging Trends in Information Systems** module at the University of the Western Cape, this project applies computer vision and deep learning to automate waste classification across seven categories: paper, glass, cardboard, metal, plastic, e-waste, and medical waste. The goal was to simulate a deployment-ready model for smart recycling facilities.

## The Challenge

A waste classifier needs to handle real-world noise: varied image angles, inconsistent lighting, partially obscured items, and diverse appearances within the same category, all while running efficiently on consumer hardware with no CUDA GPU.

## Approach

### Dataset

A publicly available dataset of ~17,000 images, evenly distributed across the seven waste categories, served as the training foundation. The balanced split ensured no single class dominated the model's learning.

### Preprocessing

- Images resized to **224 × 224px** for consistency and compute efficiency
- Pixel values normalised using ImageNet mean and standard deviation to align with pretrained expectations
- Dataset split: **70% train / 15% validation / 15% test**

### Model

I used **MobileNetV2** with **ImageNet pretrained weights** via transfer learning in PyTorch. MobileNetV2 was the right choice here; its lightweight architecture was designed for resource-constrained environments, making it suitable for training on an M2 MacBook without a CUDA GPU, while still delivering strong feature extraction when fine-tuned.

The main engineering challenge was finding the right balance between freezing pretrained layers (preserving general visual features) and unfreezing enough to adapt to waste-specific patterns. Learning rate tuning and regularization were critical to prevent overfitting.

## Results

The model achieved **82.5% accuracy** and an **82.6% F1 score** across all seven waste categories. The near-identical figures indicate a well-balanced model with consistent performance across every class, not just high accuracy on the dominant ones.

## Key Engineering Decisions

- **Transfer learning over training from scratch:** dramatically reduced training time and improved baseline accuracy
- **MobileNetV2 over heavier architectures:** enabled full training locally without cloud compute
- **Balanced dataset:** prevented class imbalance from skewing predictions toward common waste types

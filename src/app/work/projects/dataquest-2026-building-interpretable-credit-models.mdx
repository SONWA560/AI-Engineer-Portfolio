## Situation

A retail lending company needed an interpretable, auditable credit risk model that could be defended to risk managers, business stakeholders, and South African regulators under the **National Credit Act (NCA §61)**, **POPIA (§71)**, and **PEPUDA**. Black-box models (Random Forest, XGBoost, Neural Networks) were explicitly ruled out despite their superior accuracy.

The team was given a simulated loan book of ~121K applicants with 25 features, a baseline logistic regression at AUC 0.68, and a LightGBM ceiling of AUC 0.82 (for reference only). The constraint: **the final model must be logistic regression** — no non-linear models allowed.

## Task

The project had three deliverables:

1. **Research & Interactive EDA Tool** — Research machine learning concepts relevant to credit modelling and build an interactive Streamlit application for univariate/bivariate exploration, including WoE/IV analysis and data quality reporting.

2. **Improved Logistic Regression Model** — Through feature engineering alone (WoE encoding, interaction terms, regularisation), close the gap between the baseline logistic regression and the LightGBM ceiling while maintaining full interpretability.

3. **Business Decision Dashboard** — Build a dashboard enabling business users to reason about the approval volume vs. risk trade-off, understand precision/recall in lending context, and configure decision thresholds.

Additionally, the team produced a complete academic research paper covering GLM vs. non-linear theory, WoE/IV mathematics, and South African regulatory compliance with a full AI usage transparency reflection.

## Action

### Research & Methodology

The team authored a research paper examining the mathematical and regulatory foundations of retail credit scorecard development. Key contributions:

- **Structural comparison** of Generalised Linear Models (smooth, monotonic, additive log-odds) versus non-linear ensemble methods (jagged, step-wise, non-parametric hyper-rectangle partitions)
- **Interpretability vs. complexity trade-off** — GLMs provide fully deterministic, auditable coefficients where each feature's direction and magnitude is directly traceable, while non-linear models sacrifice a verifiable formula for marginal accuracy gains
- **WoE/IV framework** — Weight of Evidence encoding guarantees monotonic linear relationships between transformed features and log-odds, handles missing data naturally via dedicated bins, and enables logistic regression to process non-linear credit patterns without breaking linearity assumptions. Information Value provides an automated, low-overhead feature selection filter
- **Regulatory analysis** under NCA §61(1) (prohibited discrimination), NCA §61(7) (reverse onus provision), POPIA §71 (automated decision-making), and PEPUDA §13 (burden of proof), identifying why features like `region`, `branch_code_id`, and `email_domain_type` carry proxy discrimination risk

### Interactive EDA Tool

Built a multi-page Streamlit dashboard enabling credit-risk analysts to:

- **Univariate exploration** — select any feature and inspect its distribution across the target, with automatic WoE and IV computation
- **Bivariate exploration** — scatterplots and heatmaps to surface interaction effects and subgroup patterns
- **Data quality reporting** — missing value patterns, cardinality, distributional anomalies

The EDA tool directly informed the modeling strategy, surfacing that `credit_utilisation × interest_rate` jointly amplify default risk.

### Modelling Pipeline

Five iterative versions of logistic regression, each building on the previous:

| Version | AUC | Gini | Key Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| v1: Baseline WoE | 0.5933 | 0.1866 | WoE on 9 initial features |
| v2: Extended WoE | 0.7640 | 0.5280 | 11 features + balanced class weights |
| v3: Refined WoE | 0.7872 | 0.5743 | Smoothed WoE + L2 regularisation |
| v4: Interactions | 0.7884 | 0.5768 | Added utilisation × rate, DTI × loan |
| v5: Optimised | **0.7907** | **0.5813** | L1 LASSO regularisation, adaptive bins |

### Key Engineering Decisions

- **WoE/IV encoding** normalised non-linear relationships and handled ~50% missing data on `months_since_last_delinquency` naturally via a dedicated missing bin
- **EDA-driven interaction discovery** — credit utilisation and interest rate jointly amplify default risk; engineered interaction term `util_x_rate`
- **IV-based feature selection** flagged `region` (IV = 0.0005) and `loan_purpose` (IV = 0.0009) as noise; strong predictors were `interest_rate` (IV = 0.42), `months_since_last_delinquency` (IV = 0.41), and `annual_income` (IV = 0.38)
- **L1 LASSO regularisation** prevented overfitting on 17 WoE-encoded features
- **Class weighting (balanced)** addressed the ~8-10% base default rate imbalance
- Preferred IV-based selection over automated methods (RFE rejected), and rejected SMOTE oversampling as it would complicate regulatory interpretation

### Business Decision Dashboard

Extended the Streamlit app with policy-level thinking tools:

- **Volume vs. Risk trade-off** — see how approval volume changes across decision thresholds and whether pushing for higher volume disproportionately increases portfolio risk
- **Precision vs. Recall in lending terms** — precision as the cost of rejecting creditworthy applicants, recall as the cost of missing defaults
- **Portfolio summary** at any threshold, with regional risk visualisation

### AI Usage Reflection

The team used AI tools as analytical assistants. AI assisted with syntax guidance for WoE encoding prototyping in Colab, suggested `class_weight="balanced"` for imbalance handling and `format="mixed"` for date parsing, and supplemented EDA with additional plot types (gain/lift charts, regional risk bars). The team rejected automated feature selection (RFE) in favour of domain-informed IV-based selection, and rejected SMOTE oversampling as it would complicate regulatory interpretation. The core model logic, feature engineering, and research were team-authored.

## Results

- **Final AUC: 0.7907** — closes **93%** of the gap between baseline (0.68) and LightGBM ceiling (0.82)
- **Final Gini: 0.5813** — sits squarely in the 0.40–0.60 benchmark range for a reliable retail credit scorecard
- **Strongest predictors:** `interest_rate` (IV = 0.42), `months_since_last_delinquency` (IV = 0.41), `annual_income` (IV = 0.38)
- Delivered a fully interpretable **log-odds equation with 19 coefficients** — each directly traceable and auditable
- **Regulatory analysis** identified 3 features with proxy discrimination risk (`region`, `branch_code_id`, `email_domain_type`) under NCA §61, POPIA §71, and PEPUDA
- **Research paper** covering GLM vs. non-linear theory, WoE/IV mathematics, South African regulatory compliance, and full AI usage transparency

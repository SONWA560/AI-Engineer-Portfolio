## Overview

**Simunye AI** is a bidirectional communication platform designed for deaf-inclusive conversations and inclusive learning in South Africa.

"Simunye" means **"we are one"**, and that principle shaped the product vision: reduce communication barriers by making South African Sign Language (SASL) learning and translation more accessible, practical, and real-time.

This project was built during the **Isazi AI Accessibility Hackathon**, where I was selected as one of **60 participants out of 600+ applicants**, and one of only **20 selected from the Western Cape**.

## The Problem

[Watch the Simunye AI live demo](https://simunye-ai.vercel.app)

Despite growing awareness around accessibility, many deaf and hearing people still face daily communication friction because:

- SASL learning resources are often fragmented and difficult to practice interactively
- Real-time sign-to-text communication tools are limited for local South African context
- Existing tools frequently focus on one-way translation instead of two-way communication
- Inclusion is treated as optional instead of foundational

The goal for Simunye AI was to build a practical platform that supports both **learning** and **live communication**, while staying simple enough for everyday use.

## What We Built

![Simunye AI Model Training](/images/projects/project-01/Simunye_Model_Training.jpg)

### Learn Mode

A guided SASL practice environment where users can:

- Practice signs with **real-time webcam feedback**
- Receive model-driven recognition responses during practice
- View **AI-generated definitions** to understand meaning, not just copy motion

### Dictionary

An accessible, searchable SASL library with:

- Sign lookups by keyword
- Video demonstrations for each sign
- Simple, fast navigation for learners and non-learners alike

### Communicate Mode

Two real-time communication workflows:

- **Sentence Mode:** Captures a sequence of signs and converts them into natural English sentences using AI
- **Transcript Mode:** Streams sign recognition output in real time for live conversational support

## AI System Highlights

- Computer vision-based SASL recognition pipeline using webcam input
- Sequential sign interpretation to support sentence-level meaning
- AI-assisted language generation for clearer, natural sentence output
- Product design centered on accessibility and inclusive interaction patterns

## Impact

Simunye AI demonstrates how accessibility-focused AI can move from concept to practical community value:

- Supports deaf-inclusive communication in learning and live settings
- Lowers barriers for hearing users who want to engage respectfully with SASL
- Reframes inclusion as a baseline product requirement, not an afterthought

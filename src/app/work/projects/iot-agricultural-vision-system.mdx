## Overview

Croply is an **edge AI vision system** for smart agriculture, combining a TensorFlow Lite model, IoT communication, and live dashboards to give farmers real-time insight into plant health and disease. Deployed on a **Raspberry Pi 4**, the system detects diseases across 15 categories and tracks growth stages without relying on cloud inference or continuous internet access.

## The Problem

Greenhouse and field farms share a common blind spot: crop monitoring is reactive, not proactive. By the time disease is visible to the human eye, it has often already spread.

- Manual inspections are inconsistent and time-consuming
- Disease is typically caught days after onset, reducing intervention effectiveness
- Variable lighting in greenhouses disrupts image-based tools
- Most farms lack the data infrastructure to make predictive decisions

The target was a system that could operate reliably at the edge, achieve **85%+ detection accuracy**, and run on under **5W** of power per node.

## System Architecture

![System Architecture Diagram](/images/projects/project-01/Croply_AI_System_Architecture.drawio.png)

The system follows a **three-tier architecture**:

**Edge (Raspberry Pi 4):** Pi Camera captures high-resolution images with adaptive exposure control. Frames are preprocessed locally (resize, normalize, augment) before being passed to a TFLite model running INT8 quantization. Results are published over MQTT via `paho-mqtt`.

**Communication Layer:** Eclipse Mosquitto broker with TLS encryption and QoS level 1 for reliable delivery. Topic structure: `farm/greenhouse1/sensor01/telemetry`. Local buffering handles network interruptions gracefully.

**Backend:** MongoDB stores telemetry; Grafana provides live dashboards and threshold-based alerting.

## The Model

![Plant Growth Stages](/images/projects/project-01/plant_growth_stages.jpg)


A **MobileNetV2 classifier** was trained on **8,000+ labeled plant images** across **15 disease categories** using transfer learning from ImageNet weights. Training used TensorFlow/Keras with mixed precision to accelerate throughput.

Post-training, the model was optimized via **INT8 quantization**, shrinking it from **14MB → 3.8MB**, small enough for real-time inference on Raspberry Pi hardware while staying under the 500ms latency target.

Final accuracy: **87.3%**, exceeding the initial 85% target.

## Results

**Technical performance**
- **87.3% accuracy** across 15 disease categories, exceeding the 85% target
- Inference latency under **500ms** per image
- **99.2% system uptime** over a 3-month pilot
- Power draw under **5W** per sensor node

**Operational impact**
- **75% reduction** in manual inspection time
- Disease detected an average of **4 days earlier**
- **30% reduction** in disease-related crop loss
- **18% increase** in healthy plant yield
- ROI achieved within **6 months** of deployment

## Key Engineering Challenges

**Lighting variability:** Adaptive white balance and exposure correction are baked into the preprocessing pipeline, ensuring consistent image quality across different times of day and greenhouse conditions.

**Edge constraints:** INT8 quantization and model pruning brought the model to a size and speed viable for Raspberry Pi without sacrificing meaningful accuracy.

**Connectivity resilience:** MQTT local buffering and automatic reconnection logic ensured zero data loss during network drops, maintaining the 99.2% uptime figure.

**Scalability:** MQTT's pub/sub topology means scaling from 8 to 20+ sensor nodes requires no architectural changes, just additional publishers.
